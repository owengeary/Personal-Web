# Quantitative Financial Risk Management

Miller's book serves as a rigorous yet accessible guide to modern risk management techniques. It covers essential topics such as Value at Risk (VaR), Expected Shortfall, and stress testing, all grounded in practical application. The text is particularly valuable for bridging the gap between mathematical models and the regulatory environment, offering insights into how quantitative methods are applied in real-world banking scenarios.
